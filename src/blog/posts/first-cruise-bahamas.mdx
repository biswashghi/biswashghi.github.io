Our family didn't really take vacations prior to 2020.

We did go on certain trips to visit family, but if we ever went far, the farthest we went to was Toronto.
All of that changed though when 2020 and COVID hit. 

The first year or so was spent mostly at home, but we then had the time with me graduating, my mom and dads work situation being a bit more stable to find the time to actually travel.

We hit up a lot of places over these past 6 years, almost hard to count the amount but every single time we've gone somewhere I've always found it to be a fresh and exciting experience.

And this time was no different. After a last minute flight and cruise purchase by my mom, all 4 of us were on a trip to Miami for our first cruise ever.

<Figure src='/assets/images/cruise-bahamas/family-selfie.jpeg' alt="Family on the cruise"/>

We've talked about going on a cruise for a while, probably going on 3-4 years now.
But every single time the fear of the unknown always made us hesitate and the prices during peak travel season didn't help either.

So after our first cruise trip what did I think, what new insight did I gain on life? I'm never the reflecting type but thought to start this blog so that I can start voicing my thoughts.

The cruise we went in wasn't massive. It was Radiance of the Seas, but was larger than any that we've ever experienced prior.

One thing we all found within 2 hours of getting on a cruise is that we need to book 2 state rooms...1 room for 4 people was claustrophobic.
We got through it and by the end we didn't find it difficult, but knowing we were already going to spend a fair amount of our time close to each other in the cruise, the cramped nature of the stateroom didn't help.

<Figure src='/assets/images/cruise-bahamas/drink.jpeg' alt="Relaxing on the cruise deck"/>

Our cruise experience as a family was maybe different than others. We came in as just 4 of us compared to the other people we saw with much larger groups.

We aren't ones to make random friendships in the cruise too so most of our time was spent with us 4.

Compared to other trips we did find time to spend away from each other a bit more. One afternoon seeing a rock wall and not having rock climbed in a year, I thought I'd give it a shot.
After 1 trip to the top, feeling a bit scared jumping down from the lead, I got in the mood and tried a couple more times.

Not sure this is a thing to brag about but I can now brag that I've rock climbed on a moving boat.

Outside of that the rest of the 3 days were much less adventurous. We spent a lot of time in the deck just enjoying the warm weather.

I still couldn't get rid of the restless feeling though so wasn't able to stick around too long but the time I did spend up there was enoyable.


<Figure src='/assets/images/cruise-bahamas/selfie-solo.jpeg' alt="Watching the ocean"/>

<Figure src='/assets/images/cruise-bahamas/rock-wall.jpeg' alt="Rock climbing on the cruise"/>

What I found more fun compared to just staying in the deck was just experincing the rest that the ship had to offer.

I enjoyed the food, the singing, the karaoke. Even more enjoyable were the nightly theater performances.

In watching those though, while I was hooked into the perforamcnes, I remembered the various cruise videos I've watched, mainly of the ship crew who spend their days/weeks/months in this cruise without going home for long periods.

I thought back on their lifes and how different it is from mine where I do have the freedom to travel with my family mostly whenever time allows.

<Figure src='/assets/images/cruise-bahamas/theater.jpeg' alt="Theater show"/>

<Figure src='/assets/images/cruise-bahamas/cruise-night.jpeg' alt="Night view from the lounge"/>

Outside of the cruise though, I enjoyed the ports. Nassau was a highlight.

Throughout my time traveling, I've found out that I just need a walkable place, mostly quiet that I can just walk through and experience.

I don't need big experiences, just beautiful views and my thoughts to enjoy the walk and the experience. We could have done more in Nassau, but we mostly spent it walking around the city and spending time on a beach after a long hot walk.

<Figure src='/assets/images/cruise-bahamas/umbrella.jpeg' alt="Umbrella Lane"/>

<Figure src='/assets/images/cruise-bahamas/queen-steps.jpeg' alt="Queen's Staircase"/>

<Figure src='/assets/images/cruise-bahamas/beach.jpeg' alt="Beach in Nassau"/>

I don't think Bahamas sticks out as anything too unique compared to other experiences in how I felt. 

Its unique in its location and situation due to being on a cruise, but like all others I think the cruise just was one among the many family trips we've had where we found a time to relax just as us 4.

I do want to come to a cruise with friends one day, I imagine that might give a very different experience, but the first time cruise with family was a great experience regardless.
