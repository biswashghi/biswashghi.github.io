We just finished a cruise from Fort Lauderdale through the Bahamas. Usually, when I’m traveling, I’m thinking about the next city, the next flight, or the next work deadline. This was different. For the first time in a long time, the best part was having absolutely nothing to look forward to.

---
**The Luxury of No Plans**

Since I'm usually tied up with work, having a week where my only responsibility was choosing a deck chair was the real highlight. There was no next big thing on the calendar and no deadlines hanging over my head. I spent most of the time on the upper decks just watching the water. I did try the rock climbing wall on a whim. It’s a lot harder to scale a wall when the ship is moving and the wind is hitting you, but the view from the top was solid.

**Ship Activities (When I Felt Like It)**

The shows on the ship were surprisingly high-budget. We saw one theatrical performance that featured a massive ship prop on stage. It looked like it cost a fortune to produce. When we weren't at a show, we were usually in the lounge listening to the live band. They had these huge floor-to-ceiling windows where you could watch the wake of the ship at night. It was a nice change of pace to just sit and listen to music without checking my phone or multitasking.

**Seeing the Bahamas**

When we got to Nassau, we just spent the day walking. We went to Umbrella Lane, which is definitely a tourist spot, but the colors were actually pretty impressive in person. I preferred the quieter areas though. We found some limestone caves and trails with a lot of tropical greenery that felt way more grounded than the crowded port. If a place is walkable and has some nature, I’m usually happy.

**The Family Dynamic**

Cruising with my parents and sibling as "adult kids" was actually great. You start to see your parents less as "authority figures" and more as people who just want to relax and share a dessert. Seeing them genuinely enjoying themselves at dinner was a big part of why the trip felt so successful.

That said, as much as I loved the coordinated family dinners and the chill pace, I think next time I need to do this with friends. At least then, when someone suggests a 7:00 AM breakfast or a third round of trivia, I can tell them to get lost without feeling like a "disrespectful son."
