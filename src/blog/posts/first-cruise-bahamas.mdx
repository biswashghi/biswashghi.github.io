Our family didn't really take vacations prior to 2020.

We did go on certain trips to visit family, but if we ever went far, the farthest we went to was Toronto.
All of that changed though when 2020 and COVID hit. 

The first year or so was spent mostly at home, but with me graduating and my mom and dad's work situation becoming a bit more stable, we were finally able to find the time to actually travel.

We hit up a lot of places over these past 6 years, almost too many to count, but every single time we've gone somewhere I've found it to be a fresh and exciting experience.

And this time was no different. After a last minute flight and cruise purchase by my mom, all 4 of us were on a trip to Miami for our first cruise ever.

<Figure src='/assets/images/cruise-bahamas/family-selfie.jpeg' alt="Family on the cruise"/>

We've talked about going on a cruise for a while, probably going on 3-4 years now.
But every single time the fear of the unknown always made us hesitate and the prices during peak travel season didn't help either.

So after our first cruise trip, what did I think? What new insight did I gain from it? I'm never the reflecting type, but I thought starting this blog might help me voice those thoughts.

The cruise we went on wasn't massive. It was Radiance of the Seas, but still larger than anything we'd ever experienced before.

One thing we all found within 2 hours of getting on the cruise is that we need to book 2 staterooms next time... 1 room for 4 people was claustrophobic.
We got through it, and by the end we didn't find it too difficult, but knowing we were already going to spend a fair amount of time close to each other on the cruise, the cramped nature of the stateroom didn't help.

<Figure src='/assets/images/cruise-bahamas/drink.jpeg' alt="Relaxing on the cruise deck"/>

Our cruise experience as a family was maybe different from others. We came in as just the 4 of us compared to the other people we saw traveling in much larger groups.

We also aren't the type to make random friendships on a cruise, so most of our time was spent with just the 4 of us.

Compared to other trips, we did find time to spend away from each other a bit more. One afternoon, I saw a rock wall and, not having climbed in a year, thought I'd give it a shot.
After 1 trip to the top, feeling a bit scared jumping down from the lead, I got in the mood and tried a couple more times.

Not sure this is actually something to brag about, but I can now say that I've rock climbed on a moving boat.

Outside of that, the rest of the 3 days were much less adventurous. We spent a lot of time on the deck just enjoying the warm weather.

I still couldn't get rid of the restless feeling, though, so I wasn't able to stick around too long, but the time I did spend up there was enjoyable.


<Figure src='/assets/images/cruise-bahamas/selfie-solo.jpeg' alt="Watching the ocean"/>

<Figure src='/assets/images/cruise-bahamas/rock-wall.jpeg' alt="Rock climbing on the cruise"/>

What I found more fun than just staying on the deck was experiencing the rest of what the ship had to offer.

I enjoyed the food, the singing, the karaoke. Even more enjoyable were the nightly theater performances.

While watching those, and getting pulled into the performances, I remembered the various cruise videos I've seen, mainly of the ship crew who spend their days, weeks, and months on the cruise without going home for long periods.

I thought about their lives and how different they are from mine, where I do have the freedom to travel with my family whenever time allows.

<Figure src='/assets/images/cruise-bahamas/theater.jpeg' alt="Theater show"/>

<Figure src='/assets/images/cruise-bahamas/cruise-night.jpeg' alt="Night view from the lounge"/>

Outside of the cruise, though, I enjoyed the ports. Nassau was a highlight.

Throughout my time traveling, I've found that what I really need is a walkable, mostly quiet place that I can move through at my own pace.

I don't need big experiences, just beautiful views and enough space to enjoy a walk with my thoughts. We could have done more in Nassau, but we mostly spent it walking around the city and spending time on a beach after a long, hot walk.

<Figure src='/assets/images/cruise-bahamas/umbrella.jpeg' alt="Umbrella Lane"/>

<Figure src='/assets/images/cruise-bahamas/queen-steps.jpeg' alt="Queen's Staircase"/>

<Figure src='/assets/images/cruise-bahamas/beach.jpeg' alt="Beach in Nassau"/>

I don't think the Bahamas sticks out as uniquely different from other trips in how it made me feel.

It's unique because of the setting and the fact that it was on a cruise, but like the others, I think it was just one more family trip where the 4 of us found time to relax together.

I do want to go on a cruise with friends one day, and I imagine that would be a very different experience, but a first cruise with family was a great experience regardless.
